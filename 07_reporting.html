Reporting & Compliance

Compliance under FCRA is ongoing — not a one-time event. Annual returns, event-based intimations, and change notifications all have strict deadlines. Missing any of them has immediate consequences including registration suspension or cancellation.

🚨 31 December Is Non-Negotiable

The annual return FC-4 must be filed online at fcraonline.nic.in by 31 December for the financial year ending 31 March of that year. No grace period, no extension (in normal circumstances). Missing this deadline automatically triggers: (1) registration becoming liable for cancellation, (2) the default appearing in MHA's public database (visible to foreign donors), (3) possible prosecution. The government does not send reminders.

Form FC-4 — The Annual Return

FC-4 is the master annual compliance document. It's filed online and must be certified by a Chartered Accountant:

Part A
Basic Organisation Details
Name, registration number, address, registered purposes, key functionary details, Aadhaar/passport numbers of office bearers
Part B
FC Receipt Details
Source-wise list of all FC received: donor name, country, amount, purpose, date. All receipts must be accounted — no omissions even for small amounts
Part C
FC Utilisation Details
Purpose-wise utilisation: how much was spent on each activity/programme. Admin cap calculation. Closing balance carry-forward details
Part D
Self-Certification & CA Certification
Chief Functionary's declaration. Chartered Accountant's certificate. CA's membership number, UDIN required. Digital signature of both
Part E (if applicable)
Immovable Property
Details of any immovable property acquired from FC funds: location, area, cost, purpose. Must be reported even if under construction
Annexures
Supporting Documents
Audited balance sheet, income-expenditure statement, FC account bank statements, list of projects, activity report — all uploaded as PDFs

Filing FC-4 Online — Step by Step

1
Log In to fcraonline.nic.in
Use the organisation's login credentials. The Chief Functionary must have their Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) ready — the CA also needs their DSC + UDIN.
2
Navigate to Annual Returns → FC-4
Select the relevant financial year (April–March). The system will show pending return status. Begin filling all parts sequentially.
3
Fill Part A — Organisation Basics
Confirm all auto-populated details. Update any changes in office bearers. Ensure Aadhaar/passport for all key functionaries is current.
4
Fill Parts B, C, D — FC Receipts & Utilisation
Enter every receipt transaction with donor details. Enter utilisation by purpose/project. Calculate admin %. The system flags cap breaches.
5
CA Review & Digital Certification
The Chartered Accountant reviews the filed data, generates UDIN on ICAI portal, and certifies the form with DSC. CA name and membership number auto-populate.
6
Chief Functionary Signs & Submits
Chief Functionary digitally signs the final form using DSC. Click Submit. An acknowledgement receipt is generated — save this as proof of timely filing.
7
Retain All Supporting Documents
Audited accounts, bank statements, vouchers, contracts — retain for minimum 6 years post-filing. MHA can inspect records up to 6 years retrospectively.

⚠️ The "Nil Return" Obligation

Even if your organisation received zero foreign contributions during the year, you must still file a nil FC-4 return by 31 December. Failure to file a nil return is treated identically to failure to file a substantive return — it results in a compliance default and can trigger registration cancellation. Many organisations are unaware of this and lose their registration because they thought "nothing to report = no need to file."
