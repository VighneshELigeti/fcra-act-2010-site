📚 Core Definitions

TermSectionQuick Definition
Foreign Contribution (FC)§2(1)(h)Any donation/delivery/transfer of currency (Indian or foreign), articles (above prescribed value), or securities by a foreign source to an Indian person/association
Foreign Source§2(1)(j)12 categories: foreign government, foreign company (incl. Indian companies where 50%+ foreign-owned), international NGO, foreign citizen, multi-national corporation, foreign trust, foreign foundation, NRI (Indian citizen abroad), foreign political party, etc.
Person§2(1)(p)Individual, HUF, company, firm, trust, society, other association of persons — HUF and individuals are not typical FCRA registrants but can receive FC as relatives
Association§2(1)(b)Any association of individuals formed in India for cultural/economic/educational/religious/social purposes — the typical FCRA applicant
Political Nature§2(1)(f) + §5Organisations notified by MHA under §5 as being of a political nature based on their actual activities — regardless of stated objectives

📅 Key Amendments

1976Original FCRA enacted (Emergency era)
2010FCRA 2010 — complete recodification, replaces 1976 Act
2016 Amendment Redefine "foreign source" (banks retroactively exempted); §14A voluntary surrender; electoral bond groundwork
2020 Major Amendment SBI NDMB mandatory; Sub-granting banned; Admin cap 50%→20%; Aadhaar KYC; "Foreign source" definition expanded
2022 SC Verdict Noel Harper vs. UoI — All 2020 changes upheld as constitutional
2024 SC Order Electoral bonds struck down in Association for Democratic Reforms case

🃏 Flashcards — Click to Reveal Answer

What bank and branch must ALL FCRA receipts go through first?
State Bank of India, New Delhi Main Branch (NDMB) — Branch Code 00691. Mandatory since 2020 Amendment.
Admin expense cap on FC utilisation?
20% of total FC received in that year (reduced from 50% by 2020 Amendment)
Deadline for annual FCRA return FC-4?
31st December each year — even if zero FC received (nil return required)
How early must renewal application be filed?
Minimum 6 months before expiry date. If filed on time, registration continues pending decision.
Can an FCRA NGO transfer FC to another FCRA NGO?
NO — absolutely prohibited since 2020 Amendment to §7. Both sender and receiver face violation.
Is an NRI a "foreign source" under FCRA?
YES — NRI (Indian citizen residing abroad) is explicitly a "foreign source" under §2(1)(j). Donations by NRIs to NGOs = FC.
What does "Noel Harper" case decide?
SC upheld all 2020 FCRA Amendment provisions as constitutional. Receiving FC is NOT a fundamental right. (April 2022)
What is "political nature" under §5?
MHA can notify any organisation as "political nature" based on actual activities (protests, policy opposition, election influence). Once notified, organisation becomes §3-prohibited.
How long can FCRA registration be suspended?
Up to 180 days under §13. Can be extended. Converted to cancellation after inquiry if violations confirmed.
What is maximum imprisonment under FCRA §35?
5 years imprisonment + fine + forfeiture of FC. Individual office bearers personally liable under §37.
After FCRA cancellation, how long is the bar on re-registration?
3 years from date of cancellation — applies to the organisation AND its office bearers.
Which court has jurisdiction over FCRA offences?
Sessions Court (§34). MHA or authorised officer files the complaint. Approved by officer not below Joint Secretary rank.
Interest earned on FC deposits — is it FC?
YES — treated as FC. Must stay in FCRA account chain and be used for stated purposes.
What is the minimum activity spend for FCRA registration?
₹15 lakh spent on stated objectives in the preceding 3 years (excluding admin). Verified from audited accounts.
Can FC be invested in mutual funds or stocks?
NO — speculative investments prohibited. FC can only earn interest in bank FDs/savings accounts.
Where must FCRA violations be tried — HC or SC?
Sessions Court (trial court). No internal FCRA appeal mechanism — judicial remedy is writ petition to High Court challenging MHA's administrative order.

⚡ Top Pitfalls — One Line Each

✗1NRI sending money to NGO = FC — regardless of ethnicity or relationship (except blood relatives)
✗2Advocacy NGO organising protests risks "political nature" notification — losing FCRA registration
✗3Mixing domestic funds with FC accounts (even temporarily) = violation from the moment of mixing
✗4Not filing nil FC-4 return = same violation as not filing when FC was received
✗5Transferring FC to another FCRA-registered NGO (sub-granting) = illegal since 2020
✗6Admin expenses >20% of FC received = cap breach — cannot be "fixed" by reclassification
✗7Missing the 6-month renewal window = registration lapses; FC received after lapse = illegal
✗8Using FC received for "education" on healthcare activities = purpose deviation
✗9Receiving FC in HDFC/ICICI etc. (non-NDMB account) post-2020 = violation even with valid registration
✗10Prohibited person (judge/MLA/candidate) receiving FC — no registration/permission is possible
