FCRA Registration Lifecycle

An FCRA registration isn't permanent — it's a living authorisation that can be suspended or cancelled at any point, and expires every 5 years. Understanding the complete lifecycle is essential for maintaining uninterrupted foreign contribution access.

📋
Pending Application
Application filed, under MHA review
Duration: 3 to 12+ months (no statutory time limit for MHA to decide)

What you can do: Nothing — wait. Can receive FC only after grant.

What to monitor: Application status on fcraonline.nic.in. Respond promptly to any queries raised by MHA (they can seek additional documents).

Risk: IB clearance denial results in application rejection. No appeal mechanism before the High Court except writ petition. Keep your track record clean during this period.
🟢
Active Registration
Can receive and utilise FC freely within rules
Duration: 5 years from date of grant

Rights: Can receive FC from any eligible foreign source, maintain FC accounts, utilise per stated purposes.

Obligations: Annual FC-4 by 31 Dec, 15-day change intimations, 20% admin cap, maintain separate accounts, no mixing, no sub-granting.

Watch out: Active status can be suspended or cancelled at any point for violations — don't let compliance slip during "quiet" years.
⏸️
Suspended
Temporary halt — cannot receive FC
Duration: Up to 180 days (6 months) — can be extended in some circumstances

Effect: Cannot receive any new FC. Cannot utilise existing FC without MHA permission. Foreign donors are notified by MHA.

What to do: Respond to MHA show cause notice with full facts. Seek legal counsel. File detailed response within stipulated time.

Outcome: Either revoked (back to active) or converted to cancellation after inquiry.
🚫
Cancelled
Permanent disqualification (5-year bar)
Effect: Registration is void. Cannot receive any FC. Existing FC balance is vested in the government or may be seized.

Bar on re-registration: The cancelled organisation AND its office bearers cannot apply for fresh FCRA registration for 3 years from cancellation date.

Criminal consequences: Cancellation often precedes prosecution under §35 of FCRA. Individual office bearers face criminal liability.

Asset consequences: FC-funded assets may be seized and vested in Central Government under §28.
🔄
Renewal
Must apply 6 months before expiry
Deadline to apply: At least 6 months before the expiry date.

Continuation during renewal: If renewal application is filed before expiry, registration continues until MHA decides — it does NOT lapse while the renewal application is pending.

What MHA evaluates on renewal: Compliance track record (FC-4 filing history), any violations, IB clearance refresh, activity reports, Aadhaar update for current office bearers.

Missed renewal: If even 1 day late, registration lapses. FC received after lapse = illegal receipt even if the renewal application is pending.

Grounds for Suspension — Section 13

MHA can suspend a registration by order, without prior hearing, when:

1
Prima facie violation: There appears to be a violation of any provision of FCRA, and it is necessary to suspend pending inquiry
2
Pending inquiry: An inquiry is ordered under §22 and MHA is satisfied that suspension is necessary to prevent further prejudice to national interest
3
Public interest: It is expedient in the public interest to suspend the registration during investigation
ℹ️ Natural Justice During Suspension

MHA must issue a Show Cause Notice (SCN) within 30 days of suspension. The organisation must be given a reasonable opportunity to reply. MHA must then decide whether to (a) revoke suspension, (b) continue for another period, or (c) initiate cancellation. Any order must give reasons in writing.

Grounds for Cancellation — Section 14

Cancellation is the most severe consequence. These are the grounds:

A
FC used for wrong purpose: FC was used for a purpose other than for which it was accepted
B
Functioning against national interest: The association has become fictitious (ceased activities) or is acting against national security/sovereignty/integrity
C
False information: Registration was obtained by making a false statement or misrepresentation in the application
D
Non-compliance: The organisation has defaulted in complying with any condition subject to which registration was granted
E
Post-registration prohibition: Organisation/key functionary becomes a prohibited person (e.g., office bearer joins politics, becomes a government servant)
F
Ceasing activities: Has not carried on any activity in its stated objectives for 2 consecutive years

What Happens to FC Funds After Cancellation?

🏦 FC Account Balance

The FC balance remaining in accounts at time of cancellation may be:

✗ Frozen immediately on suspension
✗ MHA can direct specific utilisation
✗ Remaining balance may be vested in Central Government under §28
✗ Funds cannot be withdrawn or transferred without MHA permission post-suspension

🏢 FC-Funded Assets

Immovable property and assets acquired from FC funds:

✗ Can be seized and vested in Central Government under §28
✗ Government may use such assets for public purpose
✗ Organisation has no right to sell or encumber FC assets after cancellation
✗ Ongoing legal challenges to cancellation do not automatically stay seizure

🚨 3-Year Bar on Re-Registration After Cancellation

Post-cancellation, the association AND its office bearers cannot apply for FCRA registration for 3 years. This effectively blacklists the organisation and the individuals associated with it from the FCRA ecosystem. If a key functionary of a cancelled NGO becomes an official of a new NGO, that new NGO's registration application will be adversely affected. Due diligence on key functionaries before bringing them on board is critical.
